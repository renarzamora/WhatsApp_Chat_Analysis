26/4/2024, 22:07 - +52 414-7415750: *1. Present perfect* 
Have – past participle 

A situation that started in the past and continues until this moment.  

Since this morning, I have eaten 3 times. 
I haven’t drunk any sodas today. 
My sister has had 4 boyfriends in her life. 
She hasn’t gotten married yet. 


*2. Present perfect continuous* 
Have – P.P - -ing 

A situation that started in the past and continues until this moment. Emphasizing the duration of the action.

I’ve been trying to convince you for days. 
Haven't been 
She’s been working very hard lately. 
Hasn't been 


*3. Past perfect* 
Had – p.p. 

A situation that happened BEFORE another situation the past.
 
When you offered me food (at 9:00pm), I had already eaten (at 7:00pm).  
By the time she arrived home, her mom had made dinner. 
We hadn’t ever heard of this band before. 


*4. Past perfect continuous* 

Had – p.p. - -ing 

A situation that happened BEFORE another situation the past.  Emphasizing the duration of the action.

I had been looking for my cat for hours before he appeared at the front door. 


*5. Future perfect* 
Will – have – p.p.  

A situation that's gonna happen BEFORE another situation in the future.

By the time I turn 36, I will have lived in different countries. 

By the time my sister gets married, she will have had 4 or more boyfriends. 

 
*6. Future perfect continuous*
Will – have – p.p. - -ing 

A situation that's gonna happen BEFORE another situation in the future. Emphasizing the duration of the action.

By midnight, I will have been looking for my cat for 14 hours.
